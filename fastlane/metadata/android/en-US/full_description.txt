Train your brain with fun mini games that challenge your memory, focus, logic and math skills. Braincup offers 36 brain training exercises designed to sharpen your mind and improve concentration.

<b>Mental Calculation</b>:
Follow the mathematical expressions. Use the result as the base for the next calculation.

<b>Chain Calculation</b>:
Solve complex formulas step by step.

<b>Sherlock Calculation</b>:
Find a formula to reach the goal using only the given numbers and operators.

<b>Fraction Calculation</b>:
Solve the fractions.

<b>Value Comparison</b>:
Select the expression with the highest value.

<b>Bubble Sum</b>:
Add the numbers on the floating bubbles. Later, numbers blink — remember them.

<b>Quick Sum</b>:
Numbers flash one at a time. Add them up and enter the total.

<b>Anomaly Puzzle</b>:
Find the figure that stands out. Consider both the color and shape of each figure.

<b>Pattern Sequence</b>:
Find the next figure in the pattern by looking for repeating shapes, colors and rotations.

<b>Path Finder</b>:
Start at the marked position and follow the arrow instructions to find the destination.

<b>Visual Memory</b>:
Memorize the shapes on the grid, then identify the hidden shape after the timer ends.

<b>Ghost Grid</b>:
Watch the tiles light up one by one, then tap them back in the same order.

<b>Color Confusion</b>:
Tap all cells where the word matches its font color. Stay focused!

<b>Colored Shapes</b>:
Add up the points from the correct statements.

<b>Orbit Tracker</b>:
Watch the highlighted balls, then track them as they move. Select the original targets after they stop.

<b>Flash Crowd</b>:
Dots flash briefly on each side. Tap the side that had more dots. Trust your instinct!

<b>Mini Sudoku</b>:
Fill the small grid so every row, column and block contains each number exactly once.

<b>Mini Chess</b>:
Finish the chess scenario as white. Checkmate the CPU before it checkmates you.

<b>Solo Chess</b>:
Capture pieces one by one until only the king remains. Every move must capture.

<b>Lights Out</b>:
Turn off every light. Each tap toggles the cell and its four neighbours.

<b>Sliding Puzzle</b>:
Slide tiles into the empty space to put them back in order.

<b>Shikaku</b>:
Divide the whole grid into rectangles. Each rectangle holds exactly one number, equal to its area.

<b>Nurikabe</b>:
Paint cells to build the sea around numbered islands without forming any 2x2 block.

<b>Cat Queens</b>:
Place one cat in every row, column and color zone. No two cats may touch, not even diagonally.

<b>Knot</b>:
Connect each pair of colored dots with a path. Paths may not cross and must cover every cell.

<b>Tower of Hanoi</b>:
Move the full stack to the right peg. Never place a larger disk on a smaller one.

<b>Schulte Table</b>:
Tap the numbers in order from 1 upwards as fast as you can.

<b>Flags</b>:
Identify the country by its flag. Each round adds more options and the clock is ticking.

<b>Digit Memory</b>:
Memorize a digit sequence, solve a quick math problem, then recall the digits.

<b>Spot the New</b>:
Tap the animal you have not seen before. One mistake ends the game.

<b>N-Back</b>:
Watch the shapes flash by, then tap the one at the position asked.

<b>Wordle</b>:
Guess the hidden five-letter word in six tries.

<b>Matchstick Riddles</b>:
Move matchsticks to fix broken equations.

<b>Peg Solitaire</b>:
Jump pegs to clear the board down to one peg.

<b>Sudoku</b>:
Classic full-size Sudoku with multiple difficulties.

<b>Chess</b>:
Full chess vs CPU or pass-and-play with a friend.

The game has several <b>achievements</b> and each exercise has <b>medals</b> to keep you motivated.

No tracking. <b>Open source</b>.
